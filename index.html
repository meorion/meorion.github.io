A short story is one that you read and write in one sitting. In the modern age that has gotten to the point of one blink. It is called flash fiction. The words blink their "money maker" at you. They blink all that they have, and you receive and respond. Boredom is the worse response. This is where we get the great modern sensiblity of how new things are named (films, startups, babies, NGOs, ad infinitum). What word or combo of words (the shorter the better) causes one to pause? What words aren't boring? If words aren't boring then neither is our {doesn't matter}. What if we had {content} with no boring {thing}. I'm not that clever. I don't have an industry, a big budget, a skid stained intern greasing the groove for my ideas. Better yet, the ideas given to me by said skid stained intern that i have delegated these grunt creative tasks to. No, I just have my fingers, thumbs[fn:1], and a brain. These are my only minions. The flash fiction equivalent would be a sentence with no boring words. A sentence telling a story with no boring letters. No stray tilda nor her frisky kin umlaut: i'm baby, call 9-1-1. The modern equivalent to Hemingway's, classified newspaper ad: baby shoes, never worn. Maybe, 'A baby! I wanted cat.' a statement, not a question. can I incorporate these common sense sensibilities into my own writing. Dare I Dream? If so, could I control my dreams? An extra entanglement. To write is one things, to dream is another. To control a dream that in turn dictates my use of words and letters when I write ... automatic lucid somniatic astral inspired composition? It was a tall order. If I could be up to the task, would I?
