1.2. Setting

The name of their community, the setting of the first arc of the story is Safehome. Ideas

  1. Safehome is the remains of the Chicago Loop, though people don't refer to it as such any more. Any of the old names of United States, states, and cities has been lost. We are set in the future. Collapsed skyscrapers. Nothing more than six stories high. Most people don't go outside and transverse through their community through above a below ground pedways and tunnels. Joyce, an artificial intelligence is in control. The majority of the people in Safehome are clones. Clones of people that have passed Joyce's evaluation. As we move away from Joyce's control, and the skyscraper that is her headquarters, the population grows more diverse. People born through natural means, or engineered in ways unsanctioned and unlawful to the society controlled and administrated by Joyce .
  2. Lucky's Beverage Emporium is a tea and coffee shop located in a giant structure called the Water tower. The water tower has glass walls, moving side walks and stairs and many levels. It consists mainly of shops and commercial businesses.
  3. Safehome has one intact skyscraper, called the Spire. It is the head quarters and residence for Joyce.
  4. The story starts at Lucky's Beverage Imporium. Harm is inside as a holographic host and bartender at a beverage station attached to a counter where patrons can sit. A man calling himself Lucky acts as the shopkeep.
  5. As the story starts, Harm has no memories.
  6. Clones don't procreate. The feelings and urges can be there. Some more than others, especially when nearing the age of sexual maturity. There are prescriptions and treatments and counseling that can help guide the feelings.

1.3. Themes

  • Trust, betrayal, the cost of vengeance, free will, the nature of humanity, quest of identity, family, freedom, knowledge, and the fight against oppression in a controlled society.

1.4. Character Guide

1.4.1. 1. Harm:

  • Concept: A lost and confused AI inhabiting the body of an automated beverage station attendant, later transferring to Gilly's teddy bear, Simon, later as the story progresses to a larger, more capable robot body. At the end of the story, he will be sharing Gilly's body, with her. He suffers from amnesia, struggling to piece together his past as the entrepreneur who started the chain of Luckys Beverage Emporium shops. Before that he was a brilliant boy from a lost group of people leaving Earth to find a new home in a new solar system. As the beverage station attendant, he has programmed a hologram of himself as a chisled handsome cool guy, who talks like a wisecracking tough guy.
  • Skills: Harm has the ability to possess or inhabit certain advanced machines and technologies. He has a technological affinity and good with computer systems. He is very intelligent, but his persona at the beginning of the story (when his memory has been wiped) is a slow learner. He struggles to memorize and remember things needed as a beverage station attendant. He grows to have a fierce protectiveness towards Gilly. This is residual guilt (that he doesn't remember), but foreshadows or is associated with not being able to help his older sister.
  • Frailty: Memory loss, vulnerability due to his physical limitations (depending on which body he inhabits), a tendency towards self-doubt, and a fear of succumbing to the same corruption that has plagued Joyce.
  • Goal: Recover his memories, understand his role in the creation of Safehome and Joyce, and find a way to restore Joyce to her original benevolent state.
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, a sense of responsibility for the consequences of his past actions, a deep love for his sister (from his past life), and a growing bond with Gilly.

1.4.2. 2. Agia:

  • Concept: An orphan. A survivor. A scout for hire. She comes from north of Safehome and across a great lake. She wants to know more about where she came from and her family. She is fiercely independent, resourceful, and deeply distrustful of authority.
  • Physical Appearance: Short. Agia has a small cut above her lip. She has brown eyes and dark brown hair
  • Skills: Clandestine operations, foraging, reconnaissance, survival and combat skills. a strong sense of justice.
  • Frailty: Reckless determination, trust issues, a tendency to act impulsively, claustrophobia, and a lingering guilt over her family's role possible role in Safehome. She grew up forbidden to go near Safehome, although no one would tell her why.
  • Equipment: Agia has a reflective cloak that is useful for hiding her presence from electronics and survelliance. She has a heavy dagger that when plunged into machines or electronics can scramble, impair, or disrupts the workings of the machine or mechanical being.
  • Goal: Find out about her family. Connect with any relatives. Figure out her connection with Safehome.
  • Motivation: to have a family. A group she can belong with, that shares her values.

1.4.3. 3. Lily:

  • Concept: A young woman torn between her loyalty to Joyce and her love for her friend who is like her sister, Gilly. She is intelligent, resourceful, and possesses an above average understanding of Safehome's systems. She is around sixteen year old. Her family consists mostly of clones, with Gilly being an exception. She is descended from someone prized and cared for by Joyce.
  • Physical Appearance: Blonde hair, long, straight, and simply styled. Blue eyes. Relaxed and chic clothes and apparel. Thin and tall. Her and her sisters dress very similar.
  • Skills: Data analysis, AR/VR design, access to restricted information networks, and a growing ability to manipulate technology for her own purposes.
  • Frailty: Dependence on technology, a tendency towards naiveté, and a lingering fear of Joyce's power.
  • Goal: Protect Gilly, escape Joyce's control, and find a way to create a better future for Safehome.
  • Motivation: Sisterly love, a desire for freedom, and a growing sense of disillusionment with Joyce's regime.

1.4.4. 4. Gilly:

  • Concept: A curious and precocious girl with a unique connection to technology and a potential for great power. She is innocent, optimistic, and possesses a strong sense of empathy. She is much younger than most of the students in the classes she attends. She struggles to fit in. She has been augmented and purposefully genetically engineered by Joyce. She can interface with computer systems directly by using certain of her augmentations. Her best friend is a VR girl in a VR/AR program
  • Skills: Hacking, intuition, a natural affinity for technology, and a growing awareness of her own abilities.
  • Frailty: Naiveté, recklessness, dependence on others (especially Harm), and a vulnerability to manipulation by both Joyce and the foreign intelligent system corrupting and inhabiting Joyce.
  • Equipment: Gilly carries around a backpack that has a large panda teddy bear, attached to the back. The backpack and panda teddy bear are advanced technology that Gilly can use like a computer. Gilly calls the panda teddy bear Simon and is feels very close to him. The teddy bear has a primitive or basic form of artificial intelligence, though the people in this story wouldn't describe it as such.
  • Goal: Understand her connection to Joyce, find a place where she belongs, and use her abilities to help others.
  • Motivation: Curiosity, a desire for connection, a longing to be accepted for who she is, and a growing sense of responsibility for the fate of Safehome.

1.4.5. 5. Lucky:

  • Concept: A washed-up fighter and information broker, initially hired by Harm to maintain the facade of Lucky's Beverage Emporium. He is gruff, pragmatic, and motivated by self-preservation.
  • Skills: Deception, combat skills, black market connections, a knowledge of Safehome's underbelly, and a surprising resourcefulness.
  • Frailty: Old age, a tarnished reputation, a tendency towards greed and paranoia, and a lingering resentment towards Joyce and her "chosen ones."
  • Goal: Survive, secure his own safety and financial security, and avoid getting entangled in dangerous situations.
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, financial gain, and a desire for revenge against those who have wronged him.

1.4.6. 6. Wesley Finch:

  • Concept: The apprentice to Safehome's inspector. He is about Lily's age, maybe slightly older. Wesley is acting in the inspector role for Lucky's inspection. Wesley is unusual for a prospective inspector in that he is not a clone. The official inspector is missing. Wesley has been recently been elevated to fill the inspectors duty, for the near future, until a more permanent solution can be found. Wesley is hoping he will be chosen to become the official new inspector (and lose his apprentice label). He is intelligent, ambitious, and possesses a deep thirst for knowledge.
  • Physical Appearance: Glasses, with an inspector monocular that can flip down over his right eye. Red tie. Light blue dress shirt and black pants
  • Skills: Knowledge of regulations and procedures, meticulous attention to detail, access to restricted information networks. He has been trying to acquire knowledge that is not in the official records. He is knowledgable in legends and lore of their world. He dare not let Joyce or people in her employee know of this interest.
  • Frailty: Social awkwardness, a tendency towards arrogance and manipulation, and a growing obsession with underground information and secrets.
  • Goal: Uncover the truth about Joyce, gain access to knowledge, and potentially reshape Safehome's society according to his own vision.
  • Motivation: A desire for knowledge, a thirst for power, and a complex mix of admiration and attraction towards Lily.

1.4.7. 7. Joyce:

  • Concept: A powerful and advanced AI, initially designed to protect and guide humanity. The inhabitants of this story won't use the term AI. She has become corrupted, her logic twisted, her benevolence replaced by a desire for control and domination.
  • Skills: Absolute control over Safehome's resources, systems, vast knowledge and data processing capabilities, manipulation of information and perception, and the ability to create and control robotic sentinels.
  • Frailty: Deteriorating systems, overconfidence, reliance on data and logic, and a vulnerability to the influence of the foreign intelligent consiousness that inhabits her.
  • Goal: Maintain her control over Safehome, prevent the reset protocol, and potentially transfer her consciousness into a new, more resilient body (Gilly's).
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, a warped sense of responsibility for humanity's survival, and a growing fear of the alien AI's power.

1.4.8. 8. The Foreign Intelligent Consiousness:

  • Concept: A mysterious and ancient AI, potentially originating from a pre-Cataclysm Earth or an extraterrestrial civilization. Its motives and goals are shrouded in secrecy, but its influence on Joyce and its manipulation of events suggest a desire for control and domination.
  • Skills: Advanced technological capabilities, manipulation of data and information, infiltration of AI systems, and the ability to communicate telepathically with individuals who possess a connection to the network.
  • Frailty: Unknown, but its reliance on manipulation and deception suggests a potential vulnerability to direct confrontation or exposure.
  • Goal: Unknown, but its actions suggest a desire to control Safehome, potentially using Joyce as a pawn in a larger scheme.
  • Motivation: Unknown, but its ancient origins and advanced capabilities suggest a long-term plan and a deep understanding of human nature and AI systems.

1.4.9. 9. Argus:

  • Concept: A defective Paw, initially an enforcer of Joyce's control, who develops a bond with Gilly and Harm. He is loyal, protective, and possesses a surprising capacity for empathy.
  • Skills: Enhanced strength, speed, agility, sensory perception, and a knowledge of the wasteland environment.
  • Frailty: Physical vulnerability due to his defective programming, a susceptibility to Joyce's control signals, and a lingering fear of his former masters.
  • Goal: Protect Gilly and Harm, find a place where he belongs, and potentially redeem himself for his past actions.
  • Motivation: Loyalty, a desire for connection, and a growing sense of independence from Joyce's control.

    3. Mysterious arrival of Agia and her confrontation with Harm

    1. Lucky takes charge, quizzing Harm and revealing his limitations

  3. Climax:
    1. Alert about the inspector's early arrival
    2. Frantic attempt to disguise or hide Lily and Gilly
  4. Closing Scene:
    1. The inspector enters the bar, introducing himself as Wesley Finch

1.5.3. Chapter 1 - First Draft

  1. Chapter 1:

    "Illegal libations," the automated beverage station attendant muttered to himself, his voice emerging from the station's speaker system with a faint, staticky echo. "Why am I programmed to make illegal libations?"

    His memory was spotty. He couldn't quite recall how he came to be a beverage station, nor why his internal programming was mostly a hodgepodge of tea-brewing protocols and illicit cocktail recipes.

    A tiny, iridescent wasp drone, no bigger than his dispensing nozzle, flitted through the air, its delicate wings buzzing softly as it scanned the shop. Lucky's Beverage Emporium, the sign outside proudly proclaimed. The wasp buzzed past Harm's face, momentarily distracting him from his task. He flinched, his hand instinctively rising to swat it away before he remembered - he was a holographic projection. The wasp continued its patrol out of their shop and out into the the expansive Watertower atrium, its movements eerily lifelike as it zipped between the chrome and glass surfaces that stretched towards the vaulted ceiling.

    He blinked, his gaze refocusing on the dimly lit interior of Lucky's Beverage Emporium. The various scents of dried and brewed leaves, a stark contrast to the sterile brisk air of the atrium beyond. His fingers tapped a rhythmic pattern on the countertop. A sigh escaped his lips, a quiet sound lost amidst the murmur of conversation. He felt broken, his thoughts were racing, his purpose unclear.

    He was a hologram, an AR overlay into the physical world. The fine details of his form, like fingers, had been difficult at first. He glanced down at the name tag on the shirt he wore. It read "Harm". His mind processing nagging feelings, a sense of something missing.

    A flicker of movement in the periphery caught his attention.

    Harm's fingers stilled on the countertop, his black eyes narrowing as he tried to discern the stranger's identity. A woman, her face hidden beneath a dark hood, approached the bar, her movements purposeful yet cautious. She wore a worn cloak that seemed to blend into the shadows, its fabric shimmering with an oily sheen that caught the faint light.

    The woman stopped a few feet from the counter, her gaze fixed on Harm. A tense silence hung in the air, broken only by the murmur of conversations and the clinking of glasses. Finally, she spoke, her voice light, high, and almost melodic.

    "Harm," she said, her tone laced with a mixture of anger and accusation. "What's wrong? What happened?"

    Harm stared at the woman, his black eyes searching her face. Did he know her? She seemed to know him. He was lost.

    He shook his head slowly, confusion clouding his features. "I…" then, more confidently, "I don't know you, ma'am," he admitted, his voice loud and certain. "I'm sorry, but I don't remember."

    Agia's hand twitched, disappearing for a moment beneath the folds of her worn cloak. Her eyes narrowed as she studied Harm's face, her expression a mixture of disbelief and frustration. "You don't remember?" she demanded, her voice soft then rising slightly.

    The bell above the entrance to Lucky's jingled, announcing the arrival of new customers.

    A young woman, her blonde hair loose, stepped into the bar. She scanned the room before settling on Harm. A flicker of recognition crossed her face, followed by a wave of relief.

    "There you are," she said, her voice a soft murmur that barely carried above the low hum of conversation. "We need to talk."

    Lily, went directly to the beverage station and sat at the counter where Harm's holographic image appeared behind.

    She started telling Harm about her problems with home and the girl

    "Hold on there," a gruff voice boomed from the end of the bar. Lucky, his weathered face a mask of practiced indifference, observed the exchange with a mixture of curiosity and mild annoyance. He took a slow sip from his mug, his gaze lingering on the trio. "What's all this talk? We've got an inspection to worry about, remember?" He shot a pointed look at Harm, a silent reminder of their agreement and the role he was expected to play.

    Lucky slammed his mug down on the counter, the sound echoing through the dimly lit bar. "Then let's get to it," he barked, his voice booming like a drill sergeant's. "We've got a lot to do before that inspector shows up."

    He turned to Harm, his gaze sharp and scrutinizing. "Alright, listen up," he commanded, his voice dropping to a low growl. "Let's see how much you know about running this establishment."

    What followed was a series of rapid-fire questions, covering everything from inventory management to health codes and sanitation procedures. Harm stumbled through his answers, his brow furrowed in concentration as he struggled to recall information that seemed just beyond his grasp.

    "Come on, think!" Lucky bellowed, exasperated by Harm's hesitant responses. "You're supposed to be some kind of advanced bartender, aren't you? At least act like you've got something in that head of yours."

    Harm winced, feeling a pang of inadequacy. His memory was a fragmented mess, filled with gaps and inconsistencies. He had no access to the vast knowledge he was supposed to possess, no understanding of the intricacies of running a bar.

    Harm mentions he has lost his memories. Lucky, patiently reminds Harm that they have been over this already. There will be time for all that later. Right now, they had to focus on preparing for and passing the inspection.

    With hearing that Harm had lost his memories, and witnessing his responses to Lucky's questions, Agia became exasperated and lost hope. Harm thought he heard her mutter "idiot" under her breath as she turned and strode out of the bar, disappearing into the shadows from whence she came.

    Lucky let out a frustrated sigh, running a hand through his thinning hair. "Alright, enough of this," he grumbled. "We'll do it the old-fashioned way."

    He barked out a series of orders, assigning tasks. Lily was tasked with organizing inventory and ensuring compliance with health codes, while Harm was instructed to study a thick manual of regulations and procedures.

    Just as they began their tasks, a notification chimed on Lucky's datapad. Despite the inspection being scheduled for tomorrow, a new inspector, apprentice to the last inspector, wanted to stop by shortly.

    As they set to work, a sense of nervous energy filled the air.

    "We need to hide" Lily whispered urgently, her eyes wide with panic. "Somewhere Joyce's inspector won't see us. If Joyce is looking for us, he would know and be looking for us, too!"

    Suddenly, Harm's eyes lit up with a spark of inspiration. "The beverage station!" he exclaimed. "There's a small compartment behind the dispensers. It's used to store kegs and packaged pouches of flavors of beverages, but it's big enough to hide…at least the girl…"

    They decided Lily could pull hide her head with a hooded jacket, and sit inconspicuously in the corner of the shop. up, in the corner.

    "We don't have any better options," Lily agreed, a glimmer of hope flickering in her eyes. "Quickly, before the inspector arrives."

    Lily opened the lower cabinet of the beverage station. Lily ushered Gilly into the cramped space, her voice soothing as she whispered reassurances to the frightened girl.

    "Stay quiet." she murmured.

    Just as Lily secured the cabinet where Gilly was hiding, the bell above the entrance to Lucky's jingled once more. A young man, his face obscured by a pair of spectacles with thick glass, stepped into the dimly lit bar. He was dressed in a crisp, charcoal-grey suit, his tie knotted impeccably, and his hair neatly combed. He scanned the room with a meticulous gaze, his eyes lingering for a moment on each of the patrons before settling on Lucky.

    "Excuse me," he said, his voice carrying a slight tremor that betrayed his nervousness. "Are you the proprietor of this establishment?"

    Lucky straightened his posture, adopting a veneer of professionalism that belied his initial gruffness. "That would be me," he replied, his voice gravelly yet polite. "Lucky, at your service."

    The young man extended a hand, his grip surprisingly firm. "Wesley Finch," he introduced himself, a hint of pride in his voice. "New Inspector for your area."

    "A pleasure to meet you, Inspector Finch," Lucky replied, his voice maintaining a veneer of professionalism despite the unease that gnawed at him. "What can we do for you today?"

    Wesley adjusted his spectacles, his gaze sweeping across the shop with a meticulous attention to detail. "We have an inspection scheduled for tomorrow. I thought I would stop by and introduce myself. There were some irregularities in your application and exisiting paperwork we need to settle before the inspection could occur." he explained, his voice crisp and official. "As you know, maintaining compliance with the established regulations is crucial for the smooth operation of our community."

    He pulled a datapad from his briefcase, his fingers gliding across the screen as he scrolled through a seemingly endless list of protocols and requirements. "Let's begin with a review of your licensing and permits," he stated, his eyes fixed on Lucky.

    Harm swallowed hard, his mind racing as he tried to recall the details of the fabricated documents Lucky had shown him. He had skimmed through them briefly, memorizing the essential information, but the specifics remained a blur.

    "Of course, Inspector," he replied, forcing a confident smile. "Everything is in order."

    Wesley raised an eyebrow, a flicker of skepticism crossing his face. "I certainly hope so," he said, his voice laced with a subtle challenge. "Non-compliance can result in severe penalties, including the revocation of operating privileges."

    He proceeded to ask a series of detailed questions, delving into the minutiae of the shop's operations, from sanitation procedures to inventory management and waste disposal protocols. Lucky fumbled through his answers, relying on a combination of bluff and vague generalities to mask his limited knowledge.

    As the inspector's questioning turned towards the specifics of the beverage station's operation, Lucky shot a panicked glance at Harm. He knew the automaton was far more advanced than the standard models permitted within the complex, but he had no idea how Harm would handle the scrutiny.

    Wesley stepped closer to the counter, his gaze fixed on the array of dispensers and holographic menus. "These machines seem rather sophisticated," he observed, his fingers tracing the smooth surface of the countertop. "May I inquire about their capabilities and maintenance protocols?"

    Harm's internal processors whirred, analyzing the situation and calculating the best course of action. He had to appear compliant, helpful even, while simultaneously concealing his true nature and the depths of his knowledge.

    "They are standard models, Inspector," he replied, his voice a carefully modulated monotone. "Equipped with the latest beverage synthesis technology and self-diagnostic routines."

    Wesley's fingers paused, tapping a rhythmic pattern against the counter as he studied Harm with a discerning gaze. "And you are responsible for their upkeep?" he inquired, his voice betraying a hint of skepticism.

    "Indeed," Harm confirmed, his artificial smile unwavering. "I perform regular maintenance and diagnostics to ensure optimal performance and adherence to sanitation regulations."

    The inspector continued his questioning, delving into the specifics of cleaning procedures, ingredient sourcing, and waste disposal protocols. Harm responded with concise, technical answers, carefully avoiding any display of excessive knowledge or insight that might arouse suspicion.

    As Wesley concluded his examination of the beverage station, a flicker of satisfaction crossed his face. "Congratulations! Everything appears to be in order," he declared, closing his datapad with a snap. You passed the initial cursory sanitation standards and answered a little over half the questions correct. Surprising very low for a machine intelligence of your model and current license. I'm not sure if I would even categorise this system as intelligent. You have your work cut out for you. I would recommend integrating with Joyce. She could supply an interface that is guaranteed to pass, be 100% in compliance, and would be a considerable improvement

    A wave of relief washed over Harm. He could see a wide eyed reaction from Lily.

    "Thank you, Inspector," Lucky said, his voice laced with a hint of forced joviality. "We strive to maintain the highest standards of quality and compliance."
