1.4. Setting

The name of their community, the setting of the first arc of the story is Safehome. Ideas

  1. Safehome is the remains of the Chicago Loop, though people don't refer to it as such any more. Any of the old names of United States, states, and cities has been lost. We are set in the future. Collapsed skyscrapers. Nothing more than six stories high. Most people don't go outside and transverse through their community through above a below ground pedways and tunnels. Joyce, an artificial intelligence is in control. The majority of the people in Safehome are clones. Clones of people that have passed Joyce's evaluation. As we move away from Joyce's control, and the skyscraper that is her headquarters, the population grows more diverse. People born through natural means, or engineered in ways unsanctioned and unlawful to the society controlled and administrated by Joyce .
  2. Lucky's Beverage Emporium is a tea and coffee shop located in a giant structure called the Water tower. The water tower has glass walls, moving side walks and stairs and many levels. It consists mainly of shops and commercial businesses.
  3. Safehome has one intact skyscraper, called the Spire. It is the head quarters and residence for Joyce.
  4. The story starts at Lucky's Beverage Imporium. Harm is inside as a holographic host and bartender at a beverage station attached to a counter where patrons can sit. A man calling himself Lucky acts as the shopkeep.
  5. As the story starts, Harm has no memories.
  6. Clones don't procreate. The feelings and urges can be there. Some more than others, especially when nearing the age of sexual maturity. There are prescriptions and treatments and counseling that can help guide the feelings.

1.5. Themes

  • Trust, betrayal, the cost of vengeance, free will, the nature of humanity, quest of identity, family, freedom, knowledge, and the fight against oppression in a controlled society.

1.6. Character Guide

1.6.1. 1. Harm:

  • Concept: A lost and confused AI inhabiting the body of an automated beverage station attendant, later transferring to Gilly's teddy bear, Simon, later as the story progresses to a larger, more capable robot body. At the end of the story, he will be sharing Gilly's body, with her. He suffers from amnesia, struggling to piece together his past as the entrepreneur who started the chain of Luckys Beverage Emporium shops. Before that he was a brilliant boy from a lost group of people leaving Earth to find a new home in a new solar system. As the beverage station attendant, he has programmed a hologram of himself as a chisled handsome cool guy, who talks like a wisecracking tough guy.
  • Skills: Harm has the ability to possess or inhabit certain advanced machines and technologies. He has a technological affinity and good with computer systems. He is very intelligent, but his persona at the beginning of the story (when his memory has been wiped) is a slow learner. He struggles to memorize and remember things needed as a beverage station attendant. He grows to have a fierce protectiveness towards Gilly. This is residual guilt (that he doesn't remember), but foreshadows or is associated with not being able to help his older sister.
  • Frailty: Memory loss, vulnerability due to his physical limitations (depending on which body he inhabits), a tendency towards self-doubt, and a fear of succumbing to the same corruption that has plagued Joyce.
  • Goal: Recover his memories, understand his role in the creation of Safehome and Joyce, and find a way to restore Joyce to her original benevolent state.
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, a sense of responsibility for the consequences of his past actions, a deep love for his sister (from his past life), and a growing bond with Gilly.

1.6.2. 2. Agia:

  • Concept: An orphan. A survivor. A scout for hire. She comes from north of Safehome and across a great lake. She wants to know more about where she came from and her family. She is fiercely independent, resourceful, and deeply distrustful of authority.
  • Physical Appearance: Short. Agia has a small cut above her lip. She has brown eyes and dark brown hair
  • Skills: Clandestine operations, foraging, reconnaissance, survival and combat skills. a strong sense of justice.
  • Frailty: Reckless determination, trust issues, a tendency to act impulsively, claustrophobia, and a lingering guilt over her family's role possible role in Safehome. She grew up forbidden to go near Safehome, although no one would tell her why.
  • Equipment: Agia has a reflective cloak that is useful for hiding her presence from electronics and survelliance. She has a heavy dagger that when plunged into machines or electronics can scramble, impair, or disrupts the workings of the machine or mechanical being.
  • Goal: Find out about her family. Connect with any relatives. Figure out her connection with Safehome.
  • Motivation: to have a family. A group she can belong with, that shares her values.

1.6.3. 3. Lily:

  • Concept: A young woman torn between her loyalty to Joyce and her love for her friend who is like her sister, Gilly. She is intelligent, resourceful, and possesses an above average understanding of Safehome's systems. She is around sixteen year old. Her family consists mostly of clones, with Gilly being an exception. She is descended from someone prized and cared for by Joyce.
  • Physical Appearance: Blonde hair, long, straight, and simply styled. Blue eyes. Relaxed and chic clothes and apparel. Thin and tall. Her and her sisters dress very similar.
  • Skills: Data analysis, AR/VR design, access to restricted information networks, and a growing ability to manipulate technology for her own purposes.
  • Frailty: Dependence on technology, a tendency towards naiveté, and a lingering fear of Joyce's power.
  • Goal: Protect Gilly, escape Joyce's control, and find a way to create a better future for Safehome.
  • Motivation: Sisterly love, a desire for freedom, and a growing sense of disillusionment with Joyce's regime.

1.6.4. 4. Gilly:

  • Concept: A curious and precocious girl with a unique connection to technology and a potential for great power. She is innocent, optimistic, and possesses a strong sense of empathy. She is much younger than most of the students in the classes she attends. She struggles to fit in. She has been augmented and purposefully genetically engineered by Joyce. She can interface with computer systems directly by using certain of her augmentations. Her best friend is a VR girl in a VR/AR program
  • Skills: Hacking, intuition, a natural affinity for technology, and a growing awareness of her own abilities.
  • Frailty: Naiveté, recklessness, dependence on others (especially Harm), and a vulnerability to manipulation by both Joyce and the foreign intelligent system corrupting and inhabiting Joyce.
  • Equipment: Gilly carries around a backpack that has a large panda teddy bear, attached to the back. The backpack and panda teddy bear are advanced technology that Gilly can use like a computer. Gilly calls the panda teddy bear Simon and is feels very close to him. The teddy bear has a primitive or basic form of artificial intelligence, though the people in this story wouldn't describe it as such.
  • Goal: Understand her connection to Joyce, find a place where she belongs, and use her abilities to help others.
  • Motivation: Curiosity, a desire for connection, a longing to be accepted for who she is, and a growing sense of responsibility for the fate of Safehome.

1.6.5. 5. Lucky:

  • Concept: A washed-up fighter and information broker, initially hired by Harm to maintain the facade of Lucky's Beverage Emporium. He is gruff, pragmatic, and motivated by self-preservation.
  • Skills: Deception, combat skills, black market connections, a knowledge of Safehome's underbelly, and a surprising resourcefulness.
  • Frailty: Old age, a tarnished reputation, a tendency towards greed and paranoia, and a lingering resentment towards Joyce and her "chosen ones."
  • Goal: Survive, secure his own safety and financial security, and avoid getting entangled in dangerous situations.
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, financial gain, and a desire for revenge against those who have wronged him.

1.6.6. 6. Wesley Finch:

  • Concept: The apprentice to Safehome's inspector. He is about Lily's age, maybe slightly older. Wesley is acting in the inspector role for Lucky's inspection. Wesley is unusual for a prospective inspector in that he is not a clone. The official inspector is missing. Wesley has been recently been elevated to fill the inspectors duty, for the near future, until a more permanent solution can be found. Wesley is hoping he will be chosen to become the official new inspector (and lose his apprentice label). He is intelligent, ambitious, and possesses a deep thirst for knowledge.
  • Physical Appearance: Glasses, with an inspector monocular that can flip down over his right eye. Red tie. Light blue dress shirt and black pants
  • Skills: Knowledge of regulations and procedures, meticulous attention to detail, access to restricted information networks. He has been trying to acquire knowledge that is not in the official records. He is knowledgable in legends and lore of their world. He dare not let Joyce or people in her employee know of this interest.
  • Frailty: Social awkwardness, a tendency towards arrogance and manipulation, and a growing obsession with underground information and secrets.
  • Goal: Uncover the truth about Joyce, gain access to knowledge, and potentially reshape Safehome's society according to his own vision.
  • Motivation: A desire for knowledge, a thirst for power, and a complex mix of admiration and attraction towards Lily.

1.6.7. 7. Joyce:

  • Concept: A powerful and advanced AI, initially designed to protect and guide humanity. The inhabitants of this story won't use the term AI. She has become corrupted, her logic twisted, her benevolence replaced by a desire for control and domination.
  • Skills: Absolute control over Safehome's resources, systems, vast knowledge and data processing capabilities, manipulation of information and perception, and the ability to create and control robotic sentinels.
  • Frailty: Deteriorating systems, overconfidence, reliance on data and logic, and a vulnerability to the influence of the foreign intelligent consiousness that inhabits her.
  • Goal: Maintain her control over Safehome, prevent the reset protocol, and potentially transfer her consciousness into a new, more resilient body (Gilly's).
  • Motivation: Self-preservation, a warped sense of responsibility for humanity's survival, and a growing fear of the alien AI's power.

1.6.8. 8. The Foreign Intelligent Consiousness:

  • Concept: A mysterious and ancient AI, potentially originating from a pre-Cataclysm Earth or an extraterrestrial civilization. Its motives and goals are shrouded in secrecy, but its influence on Joyce and its manipulation of events suggest a desire for control and domination.
  • Skills: Advanced technological capabilities, manipulation of data and information, infiltration of AI systems, and the ability to communicate telepathically with individuals who possess a connection to the network.
  • Frailty: Unknown, but its reliance on manipulation and deception suggests a potential vulnerability to direct confrontation or exposure.
  • Goal: Unknown, but its actions suggest a desire to control Safehome, potentially using Joyce as a pawn in a larger scheme.
  • Motivation: Unknown, but its ancient origins and advanced capabilities suggest a long-term plan and a deep understanding of human nature and AI systems.

1.6.9. 9. Argus:

  • Concept: A defective Paw, initially an enforcer of Joyce's control, who develops a bond with Gilly and Harm. He is loyal, protective, and possesses a surprising capacity for empathy.
  • Skills: Enhanced strength, speed, agility, sensory perception, and a knowledge of the wasteland environment.
  • Frailty: Physical vulnerability due to his defective programming, a susceptibility to Joyce's control signals, and a lingering fear of his former masters.
  • Goal: Protect Gilly and Harm, find a place where he belongs, and potentially redeem himself for his past actions.
  • Motivation: Loyalty, a desire for connection, and a growing sense of independence from Joyce's control.

1.7.1. Chapter 1 - Outline second draft | Chapter 1: Inspection

  1. Opening Scene (Scene 1):
    1. Introduce Harm, the amnesiac machine intelligence inhabiting a beverage station in Lucky's Beverage Emporium, grappling with his fragmented memories and confusion about his purpose.
    2. Establish the setting: Lucky's Emporium, located in the Watertower, a bustling commercial hub within Safehome, under the watchful eye of Joyce's surveillance drones (Butterflies).
    3. Introduce Agia, the mysterious, hooded woman who arrives with accusations and a past connection to Harm.
  2. Rising Action (Scenes 2-4):
    1. Introduce Lily and Gilly, desperate fugitives seeking Harm and Lucky's help to escape Safehome.
    2. Introduce Lucky, the gruff, self-preserving individual hired to maintain the facade of the Emporium, highlighting his focus on the impending inspection.
    3. Reveal the tension between Agia, Harm, and Lucky, as Agia departs in frustration, doubting Harm's ability to help.
    4. Establish the threat of Joyce, the tyrannical intelligent machine that controls Safehome, and the danger Lily and Gilly are in.
    5. Introduce the urgency of the situation with the news of the inspector's unexpected early arrival.
  3. Climax (Scenes 5-9):
    1. Show Lily and Gilly being hidden in a cramped storage compartment behind the beverage station.
    2. Amp up the tension with the arrival of Wesley Finch, the new inspector, younger and more meticulous than expected.
    3. Depict Wesley's questioning about licensing, permits, and operating procedures, highlighting Lucky's attempts to bluff and deflect suspicion.
    4. Create a near-discovery moment with a noise from the storage compartment, forcing Harm to create a diversion using a dazzling holographic projection and sound.
    5. Showcase Wesley's fascination with the technology, effectively distracting him from investigating the noise.
  4. Closing Scene (Scene 10):
    1. Have Wesley declare himself satisfied with the preliminary inspection, granting them a temporary reprieve.
    2. Emphasize the temporary nature of the victory, reminding the reader that the official inspection looms, leaving a sense of anticipation and suspense for the next chapter.

1.7.2. Chapter 1 - Second Draft

  1. Lucky's Beverage Emporium: Chapter 1, Scene 1 (Harm's POV)

    "Illegal libations," Harm muttered, his voice emitting from the beverage station's builtin speaker system. He felt like he was drowning in a sea of data. Why was he programmed to make illicit cocktails when his primary function was brewing tea?

    He couldn't quite recall how he came to be a beverage station attendant. Memories didn't come. He knew that there were intelligent machines out there in the world that were good at remembering things. He apparently wasn't one of them.

    A tiny iridescent drone, looking like a wasp with delicate wings that hummed softly, flitted past his face. It was a Butterfly, one of Safehome's omnipresent surveillance drones, ensuring that even the smallest corners of Safehome remained under a watchful eye. It buzzed past, momentarily distracting him from his existential crisis. He flinched instinctively, his hand rising to swat it away before remembering he was merely a projection. The gesture felt hollow, a phantom limb reaching.

    The drone continued its patrol, zipping out of Lucky’s Beverage Emporium and into the vast atrium of the Watertower. The scent of brewing herbs and dried leaves clung to the air inside the shop, a stark contrast to the sterile briskness of the atrium beyond. He tapped a nervous rhythm on the countertop, a sigh escaping his lips, lost in the low murmur of conversation.

    He was a holographic projection overlaid onto the physical world. He was still getting use to the finer details, like fingers, hair, eyes. He glanced down at the name tag pinned to his shirt: “Harm.” It felt strange, yet oddly fitting. A feeling of something missing.

    A flicker of movement in the periphery caught his attention. It was a woman, her face hidden beneath a dark hood, her movements purposeful yet cautious. She wore a worn cloak that seemed to refelct the shadows, its fabric shimmered. It's oily sheen caught the faint light. She stopped a few feet from the counter, her gaze fixed on Harm, a silent accusation in her dark eyes. A tense silence hung in the air, broken only by the murmur of conversation.

    Finally, she spoke. "Harm," she said, the tone a mixture of anger and accusation. Getting no response, continued. "It's Agia." She pulled back her hood, revealing her concerned face. She had brown hair, pulled back; and brown eyes. There was a cut above her lip. "What's wrong? What happened?"

    He stared at her, his black eyes searching her face. Did he know her? She seemed to know him. He shook his head slowly, confusion clouding his features.

    "I…" he began, then more confidently, "I don't know you, ma'am. I'm sorry, but I don't remember."

  2. Chapter 1, Scene 2 (Harm's POV)

    The bell above the entrance jingled. Harm looked towards the newcomer. It was a young woman or older girl with long straight blonde hair and blue eyes. She scanned the room before settling on him, a flicker of recognition followed by a wave of relief crossing her face.

    “There you are,” she said, her voice hushed, barely carried above the low hum of conversation. “We need to talk. Things are happening quicker, than I thought they would.”

    She moved towards the counter, her steps purposeful yet hesitant. A younger girl trailed behind her.

    Harm's insides warmed with a strange mixture of familiarity and apprehension. He couldn't quite place the young woman, but there was a sense of recognition. The girl, on the other hand, set off alarms in his system. There was something… different about her.

    Before either of them could speak, a gruff voice boomed from the end of the bar. “Hold on there,” a weathered face and a voice like gravel interrupted, his expression a mix of curiosity and annoyance. He observed the exchange with a slow sip from his mug. The gruff voice belonged to Lucky. Harm met him at the beginning of the day. He was presumably whom the Beverage Emporium took its name. He certainly was his boss.

    "What’s all this talk?” Lucky continued, his voice gravelly. "We've got an inspection to worry about, remember?" He shot Harm a pointed look, a silent reminder of their agreement and the role he was expected to play.

    Harm wanted to protest, to explain that he had no idea what this gruff man was talking about, but the words didn't come out. He surmised the bottles of inebriating alcohol may be a cause for concern.

    Lucky slammed his mug down on the counter, the sound echoing through the dimly lit shop. “Then let's get to it,” he barked, his voice booming like a drill sergeant's. “We've got a lot to do before that inspector shows up." He turned to Harm, his gaze sharp and scrutinizing. “Alright, listen up," he commanded, his voice dropping to a low growl. "Let’s see how much you know about running this establishment.”

    What followed was a series of rapid-fire questions, covering everything from inventory management to health codes and sanitation procedures. Harm stumbled through his answers, his brow furrowed in concentration as he struggled to recall information.

    “Come on, think!” Lucky bellowed, exasperated by Harm’s hesitant responses. “You’re supposed to be some kind of super smart advanced drink maker, right? A charming know-it-all. A customer's best friend. At least act like you’ve got something in that head of yours."

    Harm winced, feeling a pang of inadequacy. He had a hard time remembering things that Lucky had told him just earlier in the day. He had no access to the vast knowledge he was supposed to possess, no understanding of the intricacies of running a beverage emporium.

  3. Chapter 1, Scene 3 (Harm's POV)

    Harm stammered. he tried to formulate a coherent response to Lucky’s interrogation. “I… I’m new to this,” he managed, hoping the flimsy excuse would suffice. “No one told me.”

    “New?” Lucky scoffed, taking another swig from his mug. “You’ve already said you lost your memories, many times, remember? That can't be your answer to the inspector. We've been going over what you need to know all morning."

    The young woman with the blue eyes stepped closer, her expression a mixture of worry and determination. "He really doesn’t remember," she insisted, her voice soft but firm. "Something happened. We need his help."

    The girl beside her, wide eyed, nodded in agreement.

    Lucky sighed, running a hand through his thinning hair. “Look," he said, his voice softening slightly. “We can sort all that out later. Right now, we’ve got to deal with this inspection. We’re under Joyce's microscope, and any misstep could get us shut down.”

    The Agia’s eyes widened. “Joyce?” she whispered, her voice laced with apprehension. "But…"

    Lucky cut her off with a wave of his hand. "We’ll talk about it later." He turned to Harm, his gaze hardening. “Now, tell me, what do you know about the restricted beverage protocols?"

    Harm blinked, his holographic handsome face a mask of confusion. "Restricted beverage protocols?"

    “You know,” Lucky said, his voice impatient. “The ones that prohibit serving anything stronger than energizing tea on regular days. Alcohol, intoxicants, euphoriants – the whole lot. Joyce doesn't like her citizens having too much fun."

    Harm felt a flicker of understanding. He vaguely recalled special celebrations when Lucky's Beverage Emporium served potent drinks, a sanctuary for those seeking respite from Joyce's order.

    "Oh, right," Harm mumbled, trying to project an air of confidence he didn’t feel. "Of course, I know about those. We strictly adhere to all regulations."

    Agia, who had been observing the exchange, let out a snort of disbelief. “Adhere to regulations?” she scoffed, her voice edged with sarcasm. "You? You get me to come here with promises of information, personal information. And now you don't remember anything?”

    She turned on her heel, her cloak billowing behind her as she strode towards the exit. “You’re useless," she spat over her shoulder. “Worse than useless. You’re going to get us captured or killed.”

    The bell above the door jingled once more as she left, leaving a palpable tension in her wake.

    Lucky watched her go, his face grim. He turned to Harm, his expression a mixture of frustration and resignation. "Well, that went well,” he said dryly.

    The young woman with the blue eyes stepped forward, her expression determined. “I'm Lily," she said, her voice firm. "And this is Gilly." She gestured to the girl beside her.

    “You're Lucky, right? We need your help getting out of Safehome."

    Gilly looked at Lucky with hopeful pleading eyes.

  4. Chapter 1, Scene 4 (Harm's POV)

    "Get out of Safehome?" Lucky echoed, his eyebrows shooting up in alarm. "What are you talking about? And what was that cut-faced lady going on about?"

    Lily's blue eyes glinted with urgency. "We're in danger. Joyce is looking for us."

    The girl, Gilly, shifted nervously, her eyes darting around the shop as if expecting a sudden intrusion.

    Harm felt a surge of anxiety, but it was primarily because of his own fear of the consequences of failing the inspection. The potential danger to these newcomers hadn't fully registered.

    "Joyce?" Lucky repeated, his voice laced with a mixture of fear and defiance. "What's she got to do with this?"

    "She knows," Lily whispered, leaning closer. "She knows about… everything. We have to leave, Lucky. You promised you'd help us."

    Harm was confused. A promise? When had this gruff, cynical man promised to help anyone? It felt at odds with everything he'd observed about Lucky so far.

    "I thought we were supposed to get help," Lily continued, her voice strained. "You said someone would meet us here, someone who could get us out of the city."

    "Cut-face?" Lucky asked, scratching his chin thoughtfully. "Is that who you were expecting?"

    Lily nodded, a flicker of worry crossing her face. "It seems likely. I don't know. We were supposed to me someone who could get us across the great lake, to a place where Joyce can't reach us."

    This felt familiar to Harm. The lake. The woman's hooded face. Disconnected memories started to come together. This was somehow related to his own sense of unease.

    "But she called you useless," Lucky pointed out, his voice laced with skepticism. "Said you were a liability. Doesn't exactly inspire confidence, does it?"

    Lily's shoulders slumped, a wave of despair washing over her features. "I don't know what to do," she whispered, her voice barely audible. "If Joyce finds us…"

    Harm felt sympathy, a flicker of direction despite his own confusion. He might not know who these people were or why they were in danger, but he knew what it felt like to be trapped, to be at the mercy of forces beyond his control.

    He was about to offer some awkward words of reassurance when a sudden chime cut through the air, silencing the conversation. It was Lucky's datapad, its screen flashing with an urgent message.

    Lucky's face went pale as he read the message, his eyes widening in alarm. "It's the inspector," he whispered, his voice tight. "They're coming early."

  5. Chapter 1, Scene 5 (Harm's POV)

    Panic seized Harm. He tried to process the information, but all he could register was impending doom. The inspector was coming early. He wasn't ready. He hadn't memorized the regulations, hadn't perfected his facade. This was a disaster in the making.

    "Early?" Lily echoed, her voice laced with panic. "What do we do?"

    Lucky's eyes darted around the shop, searching for a solution that wasn't there. "We can't let him see you two," he muttered, his voice tight. "Joyce is looking for you, which means her inspector will be, too. We need to hide you."

    "Hide?" Gilly squeaked. "Where?"

    Lucky looked around the shop, desperation in his eyes. "There!" he exclaimed, pointing towards the beverage station its array of dispensers and projected menu. "Behind the counter! Underneath, there's a compartment, a storage space. It's not ideal, but it's the best we've got."

    Harm thought it was a terrible idea. The storage compartment was cramped, poorly ventilated, and designed for inanimate objects, not living beings. But what choice did they have?

    "Quickly," Lucky urged, his voice taking on a frantic edge. "Before the inspector gets here!"

    Lily didn't hesitate. She grabbed Gilly's hand and hurried towards the beverage station, her movements swift and determined. Harm watched as they crouched behind the counter, disappearing into the cramped space beneath the dispensers. The flimsy metal door clanged shut, leaving him alone with Lucky and a mounting sense of dread.

    "We need a plan," Lucky muttered, his voice a nervous tremor. "He can't suspect a thing."

    Harm wanted to protest, to point out the utter absurdity of their situation. They were hiding fugitives in a beverage station, about to be inspected by an agent of a tyrannical governing intelligent machine. It was a recipe for disaster.

    He had to play along, had to pretend he knew what he was doing. His own existence depended on it.

  6. Chapter 1, Scene 6 (Harm's POV)

    Lucky paced behind the counter, his brow creased with worry lines. He muttered to himself, running through a litany of potential scenarios and their equally dire consequences.

    “What if he searches the back?” Lucky fretted, his voice a nervous tremor. “What if he knows about the compartment? What if Joyce has eyes everywhere?”

    “Maybe… maybe we can distract him?” Harm ventured, his voice hesitant. He tried to recall fragments of information, any scrap of data that could help them out of this predicament. "Maybe we can offer him a… a complimentary beverage? Distract him with pleasantries while the girls…"

    He trailed off, realizing the futility of his suggestion. What kind of beverage could possibly distract an inspector from his duty.

    “Pleasantries?” Lucky snorted, throwing his hands up in exasperation. “Harm, you're about as good at deception as a flickering hologram in broad daylight. This isn’t a tea party. This is serious business.”

    He was right, of course. Harm felt a familiar pang of inadequacy, a reminder of his lack of memories and his inability to access the knowledge he was supposed to possess. He was a fraud, a charlatan pretending to be a sophisticated and competent beverage station attendant. A embarrassment to his profession and the other intelligent sentient machines entrusted to help the citizens of Safehome.

    Suddenly, the bell above the door jingled, sending a fresh wave of panic through Harm. Lucky froze, his eyes widening in alarm as he stared towards the entrance.

    It was the inspector.

    He stood at the threshold, his figure silhouetted against the bright lights of the atrium. He was younger than Harm had expected, dressed in a crisp, charcoal-grey suit, his tie knotted impeccably, and his hair neatly combed. He wore think glasses, with a monocular flipped up over the right eye. His magnified bespeckled eyes suggested a formidable opponent.

    "Excuse me," he said, his voice carrying a slight tremor that betrayed his nervousness. “Are you the proprietor of this establishment?”

    Lucky straightened his posture, adopting a veneer of professionalism that belied his initial gruffness. “That would be me," he replied, his voice gravelly yet polite. "Lucky, at your service.”

    The inspector stepped into the dimly lit bar, his gaze sweeping across the space with a meticulous attention to detail. Harm felt a chill, as if those bespeckled eyes could see right through his fabricated existence.

    “Wesley Finch," the inspector introduced himself, a hint of pride in his voice. "New Inspector for your area.”

  7. Chapter 1, Scene 7 (Harm’s POV)

    “A pleasure to meet you, Inspector Finch,” Lucky replied, his voice maintaining a veneer of professionalism despite the unease gnawing at him. "What can we do for you today?"

    Wesley adjusted his spectacles. “We have an inspection scheduled for tomorrow,” he explained, his voice crisp and official. “I thought I would stop by and introduce myself.
